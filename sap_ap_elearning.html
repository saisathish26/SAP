Module 1 — AP Process Overview

The Procure-to-Pay Cycle

SAP AP sits at the end of the Procure-to-Pay (P2P) cycle. Click each step to understand what happens in SAP at each stage.

1
Purchase Requisition ME51N
Internal request to procure goods/services. Approved by relevant cost center manager. Generated in SAP MM module — AP receives downstream impact.
2
Purchase Order ME21N
Legal commitment to vendor. Contains quantity, price, delivery date, payment terms, and GL account assignment. This drives the expected invoice value in AP.
3
Goods Receipt MIGO
Confirms physical delivery of goods. SAP posts a GR/IR clearing account debit. This creates the "open item" that AP invoice matching clears against.
4
Invoice Verification MIRO / FB60
AP clerk enters vendor invoice. SAP performs 3-way match (PO + GR + Invoice). Tolerances apply — variances outside limits trigger a payment block automatically.
5
Payment Run F110
Automatic payment program selects open items due for payment based on baseline date, payment terms, and cash discount rules. Generates payment file for bank.
6
Bank Reconciliation FF67 / FEBAN
Match bank statement with SAP payment postings. Clear open items in the bank clearing account. Resolve exceptions for bounced or delayed payments.

Module 3 — Invoice Entry · Knowledge Check

Posting an AP Invoice

In SAP FI-AP, the t-code FB60 is used for direct vendor invoice entry (non-PO invoices). MIRO is used for PO-based 3-way match invoices. Click each element to learn more.

📄 What is a 3-Way Match?
▶
SAP automatically compares: Purchase Order (what was ordered) + Goods Receipt (what was delivered) + Vendor Invoice (what is billed). All three must match within configured tolerance limits. If they don't, SAP sets a payment block (R) on the document automatically.
⚠️ Payment Block Codes
▶
SAP uses single-character codes: R = Invoice verification block (tolerance exceeded), A = Free for payment, B = Manual block set by AP, Z = Block from payment proposal. Release blocks using MRBR (GR/IR block release) or FB02 (manual document change).
🔢 Baseline Date Logic
▶
Baseline date determines when payment is due. SAP calculates: Baseline Date + Payment Term Days = Due Date. The baseline date can be set to posting date, invoice date, or document date — controlled by the payment terms configuration in OBB8. Getting this wrong causes early or late payments.
💳 Credit Memos (T-Code: FB65)
▶
Credit memos reduce the amount owed to a vendor — for returns, price corrections, or billing disputes. In SAP, credit memos are posted with document type KG. They appear as credit open items and are automatically offset against debit invoices during the F110 payment run.
Scenario: An AP clerk posts a vendor invoice using MIRO for USD 5,200. The matching PO shows USD 5,000 and the GR confirms full delivery. The company's invoice tolerance is ±3%. What will SAP do?
A
Post the invoice and pay USD 5,200 in the next F110 run automatically.
B
Post the invoice but set payment block code R — the variance (4%) exceeds tolerance.
C
Reject the invoice and send an automatic vendor notification.
D
Post at USD 5,000 and write off the USD 200 variance to a P&L account.
✅ Correct! The variance is $200/$5,000 = 4%, which exceeds the 3% tolerance. SAP posts the document successfully but applies payment block R. The AP team must investigate, obtain approval, and release the block via MRBR before the invoice is eligible for payment.
❌ Not quite. SAP does NOT reject the invoice or auto-adjust the amount. It posts the document — but applies a payment block R because the 4% variance exceeds the 3% tolerance configured for this company code. The block must be manually released.

F110 Key Parameters
RUN
Run Date & ID
Unique identifier for each payment run. Format: YYYYMMDD + 4-char ID (e.g., 20260317ZZAP). Must be unique per company code. Never reuse a completed run ID.
PMT
Posting Date
Date on which payment documents will be posted in SAP. This determines the period the payment hits. Should match actual bank execution date where possible.
DUE
Next Payment Date
F110 selects items due on or before this date. Setting too far ahead risks early payment and cash flow issues. Too close means overdue items are missed.
CO
Company Codes & Vendors
Filter by company code, payment method, and vendor range. For global runs, add all company codes paying in same bank. Exclude vendors under dispute using payment block.

Module 5 — Reconciliation

GR/IR Clearing & Reconciliation

The GR/IR account is a temporary clearing account that bridges the Goods Receipt and Invoice Receipt. Understanding it is essential for period-end closing. Click each concept to expand it.

⚖️ What is the GR/IR Account?
▶
Account 199100 (typical). When a Goods Receipt is posted (MIGO), SAP debits the Inventory/Expense account and credits GR/IR. When the invoice is posted (MIRO), SAP debits GR/IR and credits the Vendor AP account. The two postings offset each other, clearing the GR/IR to zero — ideally.
🔄 GR Without Invoice (Accrual)
▶
If goods are received but invoice hasn't arrived by period-end, GR/IR shows a credit balance. At month-end, this is automatically accrued as a liability in financial statements. T-code MR11 is used to analyze and maintain GR/IR balance — old items should be reversed or written off after investigation.
📋 Invoice Without GR (Risk)
▶
If a vendor invoice is posted before goods are received, GR/IR shows a debit balance. This creates a payment block automatically (assuming GR-based IV is configured). The invoice cannot be paid until the GR is posted. This prevents paying for goods not yet received.
📅 Period-End Closing Steps
▶
1. Run MR11 to analyze GR/IR open items. 2. Clear items older than 90 days. 3. Post accruals for unmatched GRs via journal entry. 4. Reconcile AP subledger to GL using S_ALR_87012082. 5. Confirm vendor balances agree with vendor statements for key suppliers.

Module 5 — Final Scenario

Putting It All Together

Scenario: It's month-end. You're reconciling AP and notice the GR/IR account 199100 shows a debit balance of $12,000 from 3 months ago. There's a GR posted in MIGO but no corresponding vendor invoice has been received. The vendor says they never shipped the goods. What is the correct SAP action?
A
Post a manual journal entry to clear the GR/IR account directly to the P&L.
B
Use F-44 to manually clear the open vendor item in the subledger.
C
Reverse the original Goods Receipt using MIGO (movement type 102), which will automatically clear the GR/IR open item.
D
Run MR11 to write off the GR/IR balance and close the period.
✅ Excellent! Since the vendor confirmed non-shipment, the GR must be reversed using MIGO (Movement Type 102) — Return to Vendor. This reversal posts the exact opposite accounting entry, automatically clearing the GR/IR open item and the inventory posting. MR11 is only used when goods were received but invoicing/matching can't be completed — not when the GR itself was erroneous.
❌ Incorrect. Manual journal entries to GR/IR are generally not recommended and break document flow. F-44 is for AP subledger clearing — not GR/IR. MR11 is an option for old unmatched items, but since the GR itself is the error (goods were never shipped), the correct fix is to reverse the GR using MIGO movement type 102.
